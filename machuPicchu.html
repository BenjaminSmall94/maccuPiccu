Getting there

Your journey starts in the in the high valley and beautiful city of Cusco. Sitting at 11,000 feet above mean sea level, you may want to give yourself a day to acclimate. For the typical day drip to Machu Picchu you will need to wake up early, around 3:50am. You trek will start with a 2 hour van ride to the town of Olla te Tambo. From there you will have the option to catch 3 different tiers of trains along a river valley surrounded by mountains in excess of 20,000 ft. The nicest has an entirely glass roof which provides optimal viewing. This will take you to the town of Aguas Calientes. From their it will be another 30 minute van ride to the entrance to Machu Picchu. From the visitor center you can then walk 10 minutes to get your first view of the beautiful lost city of the incas

Machu Picchu and Huayna Picchu

As you stand within the complex of Machu Picchu ("Old Mountain" in Quechua) under the shadow of Machu Picchu mountain, you shall also behled the beauty and backdrop of Huayna Picchu ("Young Mountain" in Quechua). You will marvel at the beauty and majesty that was constructed by a civilization without powerful construction tools, so high in the Andes Mountains, bordering the Amazon Jungle

Llamas on Machu Picchu

Just like there were 500 years ago, when the upper class incans inhabited this hidden fortress, llamas grazed the slopes. It's possible their primordial instinctual brains remember what it was like when the Incan king would come and visit his special home at this site
